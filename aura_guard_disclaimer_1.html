01

Scope & Nature of the Application

📱

Aura Guard ("the Application") is an Android-based ambient sound monitoring and awareness tool. It uses on-device artificial intelligence — including a large-scale pre-trained audio classification engine, a spectral acoustic fingerprinting engine, and a deep neural speaker verification engine — to detect environmental sounds and voice patterns, and to alert the user via haptic, audio, and visual notifications.

The Application operates exclusively as a supplementary personal awareness aid. It is designed to assist individuals — particularly those with hearing impairments, sensory challenges, or specific environmental monitoring needs — in becoming more aware of ambient sounds. It does not replace, substitute, or supplement any certified medical device, safety appliance, emergency notification system, or security infrastructure.

⬡  Intended Use
The Application is intended for personal, non-commercial use by individuals seeking enhanced environmental sound awareness in everyday environments such as the home, workplace, or public spaces. It is not intended for security monitoring of third parties, surveillance, emergency dispatch, or critical infrastructure protection.

Users who require life-safety sound detection must use dedicated certified assistive devices such as vibrating fire alarm receivers, certified hearing loops, or professional monitored alert systems alongside any use of this Application.

03

AI & Detection Accuracy Limitations

🤖

Aura Guard employs multiple artificial intelligence-based detection engines. Each operates with inherent probabilistic uncertainty and is subject to performance degradation under real-world conditions.

⚡  Detection Approaches & Their Known Limitations
  • Pre-trained audio classification — a large-scale neural network trained on an extensive library of everyday sounds, capable of identifying hundreds of sound categories. Performance may degrade for sounds that differ significantly from the training corpus, sounds captured at unusual distances, or audio heavily contaminated by background noise.
  • Spectral acoustic fingerprinting — frequency-domain analysis that matches against user-recorded sound profiles. Susceptible to false positives from acoustically similar sounds and to false negatives when acoustic conditions at detection time differ from those at enrollment.
  • Deep neural speaker verification — a neural network that extracts a compact voice identity representation for comparison against enrolled profiles. Voice matching is probabilistic. Accuracy varies with microphone quality, changes in the speaker's voice, ambient noise levels, and distance from the microphone.

Detection sensitivity is user-configurable (Low / Medium / High). Higher sensitivity settings increase the rate of false positive alerts. Lower sensitivity settings increase the risk of missed detections. No sensitivity setting eliminates error entirely.

The Application provides a configurable cooldown period between repeated alerts for the same sound class. During this cooldown window, additional occurrences of the same sound will not trigger further alerts.

04

Accidental & False Alert Risks

⚡

The following alert mechanisms are available within Aura Guard and each carries specific risks of accidental or unintended activation:

  • Screen Flash / Blinking Alert — generates a bright white full-screen overlay that blinks for up to 5 seconds. This effect may trigger photosensitive epilepsy or cause disorientation. Users with photosensitive conditions or those prone to seizures must not enable this feature. The feature requires the SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW permission and may unexpectedly appear over any on-screen content, including during calls or navigation.
  • Camera Torch Flash — activates the device flashlight in a rapid blink pattern for up to 5 seconds. This may startle nearby individuals, create hazards in low-light driving or operating environments, cause eye discomfort, or be misinterpreted as a distress signal. Prolonged or frequent camera torch activation may reduce hardware lifespan.
  • Extended Vibration Alert — produces strong vibration patterns for up to 5 seconds. May cause the device to move on surfaces, potentially falling and sustaining damage. Individuals with certain neurological or musculoskeletal conditions should exercise caution.
  • Audio Tone Alert — injects a sequence of audible tones directly into the active audio stream. This may unexpectedly interrupt phone calls, media playback, navigation prompts, or hearing aid output. Users wearing hearing aids or cochlear implants should consult their audiologist before enabling audio alerts.
  • Audio Duck Alert — temporarily lowers the volume of other audio for up to 5 seconds. This may disrupt communications, media, or navigation prompts at critical moments.
  • Passthrough Audio — streams ambient microphone audio through connected earphones for up to 5 seconds. This may inadvertently capture and replay conversations or sounds from the surrounding environment through earphones in a shared space.
⚠  Photosensitivity Warning
The Screen Flash / Blinking Alert feature produces rapidly alternating bright and dark visual stimuli. People with photosensitive epilepsy or similar conditions may experience seizures when exposed to flashing lights. If you or anyone near you has such a condition, do not enable the Screen Flash feature.

Device & Hardware Risks

📟

Aura Guard runs as a persistent foreground service that continuously accesses the device microphone. Users acknowledge the following hardware and system implications:

  • Battery consumption — continuous microphone capture, real-time signal processing, and on-device neural inference significantly increase battery drain. Usage patterns will vary by device generation, model complexity, and sensitivity settings. The Application is not responsible for battery degradation resulting from extended use.
  • Processor and thermal load — sustained on-device AI inference generates substantial CPU and neural processing unit (NPU) load, which may cause device heating. Thermal throttling by the operating system may reduce detection reliability during extended sessions.
  • Microphone exclusivity — while the service is active, microphone access may conflict with other applications including voice calls, video conferencing, voice assistants, and accessibility services. The Application uses Acoustic Echo Cancellation (AEC) where hardware-supported; this functionality is device-dependent and not guaranteed.
  • Wake lock usage — screen flash alerts acquire a display wake lock to illuminate the screen when it is off. Improper acquisition of wake locks may prevent the device from entering a sleep state under edge-case conditions.
  • System overlay — the Application may draw over other apps when flash alerts are active. This overlay is non-interactive and will dismiss automatically after the alert duration.
  • Camera hardware — repeated torch blink activations place wear on the LED flash hardware. The Application is not liable for camera flash hardware damage resulting from use.

Privacy & Microphone Use

🎙

Aura Guard requires continuous microphone access to function. The following privacy principles govern audio data handling:

⬡  On-Device Processing Only
All audio capture, analysis, and classification occurs entirely on the user's device. No raw audio, spectral data, classification results, voice embeddings, or any derivative audio information is transmitted to any remote server, cloud service, third-party API, or the application developer at any time.
  • Audio buffers captured by the microphone are processed in real-time and are not persisted to device storage in their raw form beyond a short ring buffer used for ambient logging purposes.
  • Ambient Detection Log entries store a brief audio snapshot alongside detection metadata (sound category, detection method, confidence score, timestamp). These logs reside exclusively in the app's private internal storage and are not accessible to other applications.
  • A persistent foreground notification is displayed at all times when the monitoring service is running, in compliance with Android foreground service requirements, to ensure transparency of microphone use.
  • The user may stop microphone capture at any time by disabling the service from within the Application or through Android system settings.
  • Users are solely responsible for ensuring that use of the Application complies with local privacy laws concerning the capture of audio in shared, semi-public, or public spaces where other individuals may be present.
⚡  Third-Party Recordings Warning
Recording conversations or audio of third parties without their consent may constitute a criminal offence under applicable wiretapping, eavesdropping, or privacy statutes in your jurisdiction. Aura Guard is designed solely for personal environmental sound awareness and must not be used to record, monitor, or profile other individuals without their explicit informed consent.
07

Voice & Biometric Data

🧠

Aura Guard's deep neural speaker verification engine and spectral acoustic fingerprinting engine allow users to enroll custom voice and sound profiles. This involves the creation and storage of biometric-derived data.

  • Voice identity profiles — during enrollment, the Application captures audio samples, extracts a compact numerical voice identity representation using an on-device neural network, and stores this representation in the device's private internal storage. Raw audio recordings from enrollment are not retained after the identity representation is computed.
  • Acoustic sound fingerprints — user-recorded sound profiles are stored as numerical feature vectors derived from frequency-domain analysis in the device's private internal storage. Raw audio captures used during fingerprint recording are processed in memory only.
  • All biometric-derived data is stored locally on-device and is never transmitted externally. The user retains full control and may delete any profile from within the Application at any time.
  • In jurisdictions where voice prints, speaker identity representations, or audio fingerprints constitute biometric information under applicable law (including but not limited to BIPA — Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act, GDPR — General Data Protection Regulation, or equivalent), the user is solely responsible for compliance with any applicable consent, notification, retention, or deletion obligations concerning the biometric data of themselves and any other individuals whose voice is enrolled.
⚠  Enrollment of Third-Party Voices
Enrolling the voice of any individual other than yourself without their explicit informed consent may constitute a violation of biometric privacy law, wiretapping statutes, or data protection regulations in your jurisdiction. The application developer expressly disclaims all liability for unauthorized biometric enrollment of third parties.
